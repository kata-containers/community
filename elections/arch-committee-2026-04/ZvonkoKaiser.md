Background:

I would be honored to continue serving on the Architecture Committee and further
contribute to our shared goals. I am a Principal Engineer at NVIDIA, where I
lead Kata Containers and Confidential Containers efforts across the full
deployment spectrum, from edge and embedded systems to `hyperscale` data centers
and public cloud platforms. Over the last four years I have been a sustained
technical contributor to Kata, and I remain as committed to its long-term
success today as when I started.

My career spans low-level systems work across demanding environments: robotics
at the Fraunhofer Institute, automotive research at Mercedes-Benz, mainframe and
blade server infrastructure at IBM, and cloud-native and Kubernetes roles at Red
Hat and NVIDIA. Working across that range has taught me to think about Kata the
same way: it has to be correct at every layer, not just convenient at the top.
Unnecessary complexity is a liability, not a feature.

Within Kata, my contributions have focused on expanding what the project can do
at the hardware boundary. I introduced the virtualization reference architecture
to support GPU workloads, including GPUDirect `RDMA` and GPUDirect Storage, and
added GPU, vGPU (time-sliced and MIG-backed), and confidential GPU capabilities.
I serve on the Confidential Containers TSC, have contributed to full-stack
attestation pipelines, and have driven the integration of hardware TEEs into
production-grade Kata deployments. I also chair the Kubernetes WG AI
Integration.

One of my current focus areas is agent sandboxing, using Kata as the enforcement
boundary for AI agents operating in untrusted or adversarial execution contexts.
I have presented this work publicly at OpenInfra Live. The core principle is
simple: VMM and kata-agent signals are ground truth, and anything the agent
reports must be treated as untrusted. Security properties have to be grounded in
mechanisms, not assumptions.

I actively promote Kata and Confidential Containers at major industry venues,
including keynotes at the OpenInfra Summit, KubeCon, and the Confidential
Computing Summit. Getting this technology in front of the right audiences is as
important as building it.

Earlier in my career I focused on making GPUs a first-class citizen in
Kubernetes. That work is largely done. My focus now is to do the same for Kata,
Confidential Containers, and confidential GPU computing: not as add-ons or
special cases, but as native, well-integrated parts of the Kubernetes ecosystem.

If given the opportunity to continue serving, I will advocate for technically
grounded, practical decisions, open community engagement, and the kind of
transparency that keeps a security-critical project trustworthy. Thank you for
considering my nomination.

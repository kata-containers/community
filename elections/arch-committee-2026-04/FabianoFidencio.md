Background:

I am a Software Engineer, a tattoo canvas for abstract art, a music addict, a
goats and pigs instagram follower, and a big fan of old video games.

In the past 6 years I dedicated a huge chunk of my professional and personal
time contributing to the Kata Containers project and community, with a strong
focus on helping the project growing, regardless of my employer at the time.

My involvement with Kata Containers has been intense as I've been involved in
pretty much every part of the project, as well as in upstream projects that
Kata Containers interacts with.  Moreover, I've also served the community as
an Architecture Committee member several years ago, for several years in a row,
but at the time I stepped out as I was not able to provide the community the
dedication it deserved.

In the enterprise world, I have worked on and left a considerable footprint on
Red Hat's product using OpenShift, on Intel's TDX support and their commitment
to the project, and most recently on NVIDIA's work to bring in confidential
computing and workload isolation as part of the AI infrastructure.

In the community space, I also have helped on a lot of different parts of the
project. connected people to have their problems solved, mentored both
newcomers and students into our community, led Architecture Committee meetings,
led vPTG's Kata Containers session, have publicly spoken about the project, and
mentored and guided folks to become proficient in the project (and nowadays I
see some of those either as part of the Architecture Committee or aspiring to
be part of the Architecture Committee).  I'm fully hands-on on code-reviewing,
development, CI, always focusing on easing the adoption of the project.  I have
been managing past and current releases, and have been working as much as
possible to grow this community in a healthy way.

My main motivations are still to increase Kata Containers adoption in the
industry, while ensuring the project is stable and easily consumable.  This is
more important now than ever as new players are adopting and contributing to
the Kata Containers project.

To make it possible, I plan to continue working in the integration bits, while
connecting and facilitating the communication between the Kata Containers
community and the communities that I am part of.

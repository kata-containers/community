## Candidacy

#### Who I am

I'm a mathematician, by training, who stumbled into software development after graduation.
By now, I've worked at various large corporations on all sorts of different software: calibration of electron microscopes, computer vision and machine learning, workflow orchestration on Kubernetes and large scale network monitoring.
I have a knack for clear definitions and like to design things in writing before jumping into implementations.
Regarding software, I strive to develop simple and secure systems, which are ideally extensible.

Together with my family, I live in Augsburg, Germany.
Fitting for someone who grew up in the Bavarian alps, I am a certified ski and snowboard instructor.
That, and my long term tenure as a boy scout leader, maybe qualifies me most for working in open source.

#### Why you might know me

I've been working with and on Kata since early 2024, shortly after joining Edgeless Systems.
You might have noticed me being active on the Kata and CoCo Slacks, or as a reviewer for the `genpolicy` sub-project.
I presented more than once in the CoCo community call and represented Kata at the last OpenInfra summit and at FOSDEM.

There are a couple of Kata RFCs with my name on them, focusing on Kata for confidential computing and general extensibility of the project.
I'm also trying my best to help out with other parts, like runtime-rs and, honestly, wherever I get tagged in a PR.

As of recently, I'm also involved in the IETF SEAT WG and a little bit in CCC Attestation.

#### Why you might want to elect me

Software security is dear to my heart, and I'd love to align the community behind that goal.
I am very much aware that Kata is not a one-trick-pony, and balancing the various needs of users and downstream vendors is important to me.
In the same vein, I would like for the community to develop a threat model and a set of security guarantees that Kata can (and cannot) provide.

I also take some pride into being nice and welcoming to users and contributors.
Upholding the code of conduct is table stakes, of course, but I also appreciate the small opportunities for not annoying people, a recent example being our approach towards issue staleness.

I want to maintain Kata's position as the default tool for VM isolation in Kubernetes, and make it modular enough to support the various user requirements while staying lean at the core.
Eventually, once things settle around Kata 4.0, I would like to start an overhaul of the Kata build system:
While it served us well over the years, I feel that our day and age has much to offer regarding reproducible builds and developer tooling, which we should take advantage of.

#### Why you might want to elect someone else

I depend on CI to do the heavy lifting and rarely ran an e2e test myself.
In reviews and RFC discussions I can sometimes be pedantic or outright annoying, leaning towards principles and away from pragmatism.
The usual time horizon between me starting a thing and me finishing it is measured in months, but I always try to follow through. Eventually.
